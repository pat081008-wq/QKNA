Interpretation Guide
Kriging Efficiency (KE)
• KE > 60% — Good. Low over-smoothing.
• KE 30–60% — Moderate. Acceptable in practice.
• KE < 30% — Poor. High over-smoothing likely.
KE = 1 − σ²_K / σ²_data, expressed as a percentage. Values may be negative (SK-style kriging plan may under-perform).
Slope of Regression
• Slope ≥ 0.9 — Excellent. Minimal conditional bias.
• Slope 0.7–0.9 — Acceptable. Some over-smoothing.
• Slope < 0.7 — Poor. Significant over-smoothing.
Slope = Cov(Z*, Z) / Var(Z*). Slope = 1 implies unbiased estimation. Slope < 1 indicates over-smoothing of high and low grades.
Desurveying Method
Minimum Curvature — Assumes the borehole follows a smooth arc between survey stations. Most accurate for curved holes.

Tangential — Uses the survey direction at the end of each segment. Fast but less accurate for highly deviated holes.

Average Angle — Averages the dip/azimuth at the top and bottom of each segment. Good general-purpose method.
